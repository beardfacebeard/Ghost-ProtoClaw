## CHANGELOG — v2 (TikTok Shop setup corrected — superseded by v3)

The original v1 script for Video 1 listed Stripe and Resend as setup steps. That was wrong for a pure-TikTok-Shop deployment because:

- TikTok Shop runs its own payment gateway. Buyers pay TikTok directly. TikTok holds funds and ACH-transfers payouts to a seller-linked bank account on a settlement schedule (Standard 8 days, can accelerate to 1 day at higher SPS).
- TikTok handles all order-related buyer emails. Sellers do not receive buyer email addresses (only masked addresses for shipping), so a transactional email service like Resend has nothing to nurture from the Shop side.

This was the v2 fix. v3 (above) makes it official — the template now reflects this in code: `requiredIntegrations: ["social_media_mcp"]` and the digital-ladder stack lives in an optional addon.

# WHAT YOU'RE MISSING — LAUNCH CHECKLIST

You asked what's missing. Here's everything I'd want locked in before publishing either video. Treat this like a pre-flight.

## A. Conversion infrastructure

- **UTM-tagged landing pages.** `ghostprotoclaw.com/tiktok-shop` and `/faceless-youtube` should be dedicated landing pages, not your homepage. The hero on each = the same hook the video opens with. Add `?utm_source=youtube&utm_medium=video&utm_campaign=...` to every link in the description so you can see which video drives signups.
- **Lead capture before the buy button.** Add an email gate that offers something the video implied — "The 12-Agent TikTok Shop Setup Checklist (PDF)" or "The Faceless YouTube 7-Day Buffer Calendar Template." 30-50% of viewers won't buy on first watch. Capture them, retarget them, email them.
- **Pixel and conversion tracking.** Meta Pixel + Google Tag for retargeting cold traffic from YouTube. A conversion event when somebody clicks deploy. Without this, you don't know which video, thumbnail, or hook is making money.
- **A "watch the video" CTA on the homepage.** Most landing-page video views come from the homepage. Make sure the homepage hero plays your landing-page video by default, and the template-specific landing pages play the relevant template demo.

## B. Trust & social proof

- **At least one real revenue screenshot or template-output sample.** A real CEO daily digest, a real revenue chart, a real "this video was made by the studio" example. Faceless YouTube viewers especially need to see a real channel running on the template. If you don't have one yet, build a public demo channel and reference it.
- **A founder bio bar.** Two sentences on you, why you built this, how long Ghost ProtoClaw has been live. Cuts the "is this a scam" reflex in half.
- **One testimonial card per template page.** Even from beta users. A face, a name, a quote, ideally a screenshot of their dashboard.

## C. Compliance / disclaimers

- **Income disclaimer** in the description and on the landing page. "Results vary. Not a guarantee of income." This is not optional.
- **AI content disclosure** language in the videos. The template handles labeling on TikTok and YouTube — but the marketing video itself should mention this so you don't get accused of hiding it.
- **FTC affiliate disclosure** for any tool you mention with an affiliate link (HeyGen, ElevenLabs, etc. all have programs — sign up; you'll make 5-15% on tool referrals on top of your own product).

## D. Repurposing (turn each long video into 10+ assets)

- **YouTube Shorts cuts.** Three per video minimum: (1) the cold open / hook, (2) the "15-minute timer hits" moment, (3) the "what waking up looks like" beat. Cross-post to TikTok, Reels, Shorts.
- **A 60-second landing-page hero loop.** Just the hook + the dashboard + the timer. Auto-plays muted on the landing page.
- **A still-image carousel** for LinkedIn / X: the integrations stack diagram, the agent org chart, the "before vs. after my day" split.
- **A blog post** with the full transcript + the integrations block + screenshots — this is what ranks on Google for "TikTok Shop AI agent setup" and "faceless YouTube AI automation."

## E. Production quality bar

- **Real screen recordings, not mockups.** YouTube viewers smell faked product demos. Use the actual app even if a few corners look unfinished — the unfinished corners read as authentic.
- **Captions burned in.** 70%+ of YouTube viewers watch on mute or with captions. Auto-captions are not enough — review them.
- **Music bed.** Nothing dramatic. Lo-fi or cinematic ambient under the calm walkthrough sections. Drop the music entirely on the cold open and the close — voice carries those.
- **A 1-minute Veo3 / cinematic intro asset** you can reuse as your channel opener. Don't film it twice.

## F. Distribution & seeding

- **Pin a comment within 5 minutes** of publishing. You wrote them — paste them.
- **Seed comments** with three friends/early users in the first hour: real questions ("how does the compliance agent handle TikTok's new AI rule?") that let you reply with extra value. YouTube weights early comment activity heavily.
- **Cross-post.** Twitter/X thread breaking down the agent crew. LinkedIn post for the B2B/operator audience. A Reddit post in r/Entrepreneur, r/passive_income, r/sidehustle, r/youtubers — read each sub's self-promo rules first.
- **Email your list** the day each video drops with a 1-line "I just published the template demo, here's the deploy link."

## G. Analytics & iteration

- **Watch retention curve** on each video at 24 hours, 7 days, 30 days. The cliff (where viewers drop) is your next edit point. Retitle / reupload if retention dies in the first 30 seconds.
- **Title + thumbnail A/B test** using YouTube's built-in test feature. Run the title the algorithm responds to first, not the one *you* like first.
- **Conversion rate by traffic source.** YouTube viewer → site click → email captured → deploy. Each step should have a number. If YouTube → site click is great but click → deploy is bad, the landing page is the leak — not the video.

## H. Things that will come back to bite you

- **Don't promise revenue numbers** in the videos unless you can show a real, recent dashboard. Use process language ("the team runs the workflow") not outcome language ("you'll make $10K"). Outcome claims invite refunds, chargebacks, and FTC mail.
- **Don't show competitor logos disrespectfully.** OpenClaw is your "old, hard way" — fine — but speak about it with respect. Your buyer respects them. Disrespecting OpenClaw makes you look small.
- **Don't gate the videos.** Both should be 100% public on YouTube AND embedded on the landing page. The video IS the funnel. Don't hide your top-of-funnel asset.
- **Don't ship without a refund policy.** A clean 7-day or 14-day refund cuts the buying friction and is a stronger close than any guarantee guru voice.

Record a **third short video** — 90 seconds, just you, no screen — that lives ABOVE both demo videos on the landing page:
